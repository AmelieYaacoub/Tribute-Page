Queen of Fado
Amália Rodrigues, 1969

Amália da Piedade Rebordão Rodrigues, was a Portuguese fadista (fado singer) and actress.
Known as the 'Rainha do Fado' ("Queen of Fado"), Rodrigues was instrumental in popularising fado worldwide and travelled internationally throughout her career.
Amália remains the best-selling Portuguese artist in history.

LIFE & DEATH OF A QUEEN

  • 1920 - Rodrigues was one of ten children of a poor family from Fundão and was born in Lisbon, 23th of July.
  • 1939 - She began her career as a fado singer in the nightclub Retiro da Severa.
  • 1940 - Rodrigues married the amateur guitarist Francisco da Cruz. The marriage ended after three years.
  • 1944/45 - Tours led her to Brazil, where her first recordings was made. In the following decades she recorded countless records (the number is estimated to be over 170) and appeared in about a dozen films. Tours took her around the world.
  • 1947 - She had her first acting role in the movie Capas negras.
  • 1950 - She made her international breakthrough with the song Coimbra, which has had worldwide success as Avril au Portugal and then as April in Portugal.
  • 1961 - She married the Brazilian engineer César Seabra, to whom she was married until his death in 1997.
  • 1967 - She was awarded the MIDEM Prize in Cannes.
  • 1998 - Last public appearance at the world exhibition Expo 98 as part of a tribute to her.
  • 1999 - Rest in piece, 6th of October.
